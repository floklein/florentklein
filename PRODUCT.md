# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers (frontend / lead-frontend roles), mostly French-speaking, evaluating Florent Klein for a position. They arrive with a specific question — *is this person worth interviewing?* — and skim under time pressure across many candidates. They need to judge seniority, technical range, and craft quickly, then have an easy way to make contact. Secondary visitors (peers, the occasional client) may go deeper, but the design optimizes for the recruiter's fast credibility read.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio at `florentklein.dev` whose job is to land Florent a frontend/lead-frontend role. It makes the case through three things: a clear experience timeline (currently Lead Frontend at Dametis, a greentech SaaS), a curated set of personal projects shown with real screenshots and honest tech stacks, and an AI assistant that lets a visitor interrogate his background conversationally. Success = a recruiter leaves convinced of his seniority and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

**Precise, modern, confident.** The voice is a senior engineer who doesn't oversell — restraint reads as mastery. French copy, direct and unembellished. Quiet confidence: the work and the craft of the site itself carry the argument, rather than adjectives about it. The AI assistant is a **signature feature** — a deliberate, prominent demonstration of taste and capability, not a gimmick bolted on.

## Anti-references

- **Over-designed / loud:** no heavy animation pile-ups, glassmorphism, gradient text, or style-over-substance theatrics.
- **Cluttered / dense:** no walls of text, no everything-competing-for-attention layouts. Breathing room is part of the message.
- **Corporate SaaS template:** no hero-metric blocks, no gradient-accented marketing clichés, no endless identical icon-card grids.
- **Generic shadcn / AI-default:** the site currently leans on shadcn's default neutral system; it should grow a distinct, deliberate identity rather than read as another Next.js + shadcn starter.

Reference for *feel* (quality to aim at, not to copy): the Linear / Vercel lineage — sharp typography, engineering-grade polish, restrained and purposeful motion, comfortable in dark mode.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the argument.** Screenshots, real stacks, and a truthful timeline do the persuading. Chrome stays out of the way of the evidence.
2. **Restraint signals seniority.** Every element earns its place. When in doubt, remove. Precision over decoration.
3. **Skimmable in seconds, rewarding in minutes.** A recruiter gets the gist on a fast scroll; a curious visitor who lingers (or opens the assistant) finds more depth.
4. **Practice what you preach.** This site is itself a portfolio piece — its craft, performance, and accessibility are part of the case for hiring a Lead Frontend.
5. **The assistant is a feature, not a toy.** The AI chat is held to the same bar as the rest: useful, fast, on-brand, and clearly intentional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1 and large text ≥3:1 against their backgrounds in both light and dark themes; visible keyboard focus states; full keyboard operability for interactive elements including the AI chat; and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for every animation. Meeting this bar is also a deliberate craft signal to a frontend-hiring audience.
